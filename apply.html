FAQ

  • What do these companies have in common?

    1. They are mostly coded in Australia
    2. They have 5K+ people use or visit their site monthly
    3. They display "Made in Australia" as prominently as its copyright and links to this page - http://idsclub.org/made-in-aus

  • How can we be added to the list?

    If you meet criteria, submit this form and expect response in about a week.

  • What does "mostly coded in Australia" mean?

    Over 50% of the front & backend code for the website or application was written in Australia

  • How do you verify that participants meet the "mostly coded in Australia" and "5K+ people" rules?

    For now, honor system. Maybe later, verification TBD. Violations will be made public.

  • My company is based in Australia but our technology is mostly made (coded) elsewhere. Can we be listed?

    No. Many great companies and projects are certainly being conceived and managed and funded in Australia, but that doesn’t mean they’re made in Australia. This is for internet that’s made in Australia.

  • Why the strict "mostly coded in Australia" rule?

    Because tech is coded, so made means made. Made doesn’t mean funded or managed. Like a shirt that’s designed/licensed/funded in Australia, and made in China: It’s Made in China, not Made in Australia. Made means made.

  • Is there a “Made in Australia” logo that we can display?

    We don’t have an official logo, but see "Adverb or verb? What kind of adverb or verb?" for what you should display on your site

  • Who's behind this?

    This is a project of the Australia IDs Club.

  • What's the point?

    If Australia is to thrive in the 21st century, Australia needs to make more internet. Thankfully and excitingly, Australia is now (2013) finally making some of the best internet in the world (see list above). So let’s proudly show ourselves off in the hope that everyone everywhere (including software engineers who don’t live in the world’s greatest city) know what internet is made in Australia.

  • Adverb or verb? What kind of adverb or verb?

    Here are some examples:
    1. Foursquare says "Lovingly Made in New York"
    2. Meetup.com says "Meetup is Proudly Made in NYC"
    3. Aviary says "Hatched in NYC"

  • Where should I send suggestions for improving this page?

    Send to ideas@idsclub.org

常见问题

  • 这些伙伴们有什么共同之处？

    1. 他们大多是开发或营运都在澳大利亚
    2. 他们每月有1K +的人使用或访问他们的网站
    3. 他们显示“xxx用心制造/Designed with ♥ by xxx”为突出其同伴圈, 可参考其他同伴: idsclub.org/designed-with-love

  • 我们怎样才能被加入到同伴圈里？

    如果您符合条件，提交此表，我们预计在一个星期左右完成加入流程。

  • 什么是“大多是开发或营运在澳大利亚”是什么意思？

    超过50％的网站或应用程序的前端和后端代码或产品营运在澳洲完成

  • 你怎么审核 “大多是开发或营运在澳大利亚”和“1K+人”的规则？

    目前只能通过“你说我信”的信用体系，也许以后用其他第三方方式，待确定，如有违规行为将被公开。

  • 我产品营运在澳大利亚，但我们的技术主要是由其他地区开发，我们可以加入不？

    可以，目前许多创新的互联网产品是来自不同区域协作，开发，营运，管理，只要你超过50％的网站营运在澳洲，我们欢迎您加入我们同伴圈。

  • 为什么要有 “主要是在澳大利亚开发或营运”的要求？

    因为我们是澳大利亚区域的互联网同伴圈，希望更多该区域创新的互联网产品同伴能加强交流和认识，通过认识和碰撞，创造出更伟大的互联网产品。

  • 是否有“用心制造”的标志，我们可以显示？

    我们没有一个官方的标志，我们只有文字“xxx用心制造/Designed with love by xxx”，你应该显示在您的网站，为突出其同伴圈，可参考其他同伴: http://idsclub.org/designed-with-love

  • 谁在支持这个项目？

    IDs澳洲互联网俱乐部发起和支持着这个同伴圈的项目的营运。

  • 我们需要更多的建议，来完善我们的同伴圈，欢迎任何建议和想法。

    请花几分钟把你想法，分享到我们邮箱: ideas@idsclub.org
